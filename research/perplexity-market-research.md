## Thread 2 — SDVOSB / Federal Maximo Competitive Landscape

### The SDVOSB Maximo set is narrower than expected

The investigative data shows a **single dominant SDVOSB Maximo Gold Partner competitor**: **Intelligent Technology Solutions, LLC (ITS / webuildits.com)** — a Georgia-based, 20-year-old SDVOSB serving DoD (Army, Navy, USAF, Marines), federal agencies, and S&P companies. ITS already holds a DoD IBM Maximo contract. This is Grable's direct, like-for-like competitor.

Beyond ITS, the SDVOSB-certified Maximo specialist pool thins quickly. Most "veteran-owned Maximo" claimants are either small staff-aug shops (no Gold Partner status) or non-Maximo IT firms cross-selling. Strategic Endeavor LLC appears in IBM PartnerPlus but with thinner public footprint.

### The federal market size is significant and accelerating

- **FY2025 SDVOSB federal spend:** ~$28.6 billion across ~52,000 contract actions (Fed-Spend).
- **Cumulative SDVOSB spend benchmark:** $31.9 billion noted by SupplierDiversity.com (cumulative/aggregate framing).
- **Statutory goal:** 5% of all federal contracting dollars to SDVOSBs.
- **IBM supplier diversity reorientation:** IBM has explicitly refocused on small businesses and veteran-led companies — a tailwind for SDVOSB Maximo partners.

### Capability statement structure that wins

The winning federal Maximo capability statement is **one page, never two**, with eight non-negotiable blocks:

1. Company name, UEI (DJKXNKLXRAF7), CAGE (7CW57), DUNS legacy, contact.
2. Core competencies — **specific**, not "IT services." For Grable: "MAS 9 migration on AWS GovCloud," "FedRAMP-eligible Maximo hosting," "IL2-IL6 compliant asset management."
3. Differentiators (Gold Partner, 800M+ sqft, IL2-IL6, Caesars + Xerox + Sony + Genentech).
4. Certifications block — SDVOSB, IBM Gold Partner, FedRAMP-aligned, ISO 27001, NIST 800-171.
5. NAICS codes — primary 541512 (Computer Systems Design), with 541519 (Other Computer Related Services / SaaS hosting) and 541611 (Management Consulting) as secondaries.
6. Past performance / clients (logos).
7. Contract vehicles (any GSA Schedule, SEWP, 8(a) graduated status if applicable).
8. Logo + brand colors at top, federal-blue/navy palette preferred.

### NAICS competitive density

- **541512 (Computer Systems Design):** Set-aside frequent. Size standard $34M (revenue-based, recent SBA proposed updates). High SDVOSB density.
- **541519 (Other Computer Related):** Where Maximo-as-a-Service / SaaS-hosting offerings sit cleanly. Size standard 150 employees for ITVAR subsection. **Less crowded than 541512** — strategic positioning opportunity for Grable.
- **541611 (Management Consulting):** $24M size standard, 8(a) sole-source common. Useful as secondary code for the advisory side of MAS migrations.

### Federal contract awards visibility

The investigation hit a wall on specific 2024-2026 Maximo contract awards — SAM.gov's contract awards search is in soft-launch (since July 2025) and does not produce filterable Maximo-keyword + IL4/IL5 data via web search. **Recommendation:** A direct SAM.gov + USASpending.gov FOIA-style query is the next investigative step, not Google. ### FedRAMP MAS 9 — April 2026 is the inflection point

This is the single most important finding of the entire research. **IBM Maximo Application Suite as a Service for Government achieved FedRAMP Moderate authorization on AWS GovCloud (U.S.) in April 2026** — first announced April 1, 2026 (IBM Newsroom, PRNewswire, ExecutiveBiz, StockTitan, Govly) and formalized in a dedicated MAS announcement on April 28, 2026 (IBM, MaxIron coverage).

What it means for SDVOSB Maximo firms:

- The certification covers **IBM's own MAS-as-a-Service** offering on AWS GovCloud — meaning the floor of compliance is now set.
- For SDVOSBs, this **opens the federal market** for value-added Maximo services on top of (or aligned with) the IBM authorized boundary.
- Grable's claim of "FedRAMP High / NIST / ISO 27001 / IL2–IL6 capable" must now be carefully positioned **above** IBM's FedRAMP Moderate baseline — if Grable can credibly deliver FedRAMP **High**, that's a sharp differentiator vs IBM's own MaaS, which is only Moderate.
- Federal procurement teams will increasingly write Maximo RFPs requiring "FedRAMP authorized or equivalent" — Grable's website must answer this in plain text inside the first scroll.

## Thread 3 — 2026 B2B Enterprise Consulting Website Best Practices

### What separates top-decile from average

Five patterns repeat across every modern source (Paradigm Marketing, Windmill Strategy, Clear Digital, Grafit, Centric, Axon Garside):

1. **Outcome-specific positioning above the fold.** Not "Enterprise IT Solutions" — "Cut your manual data entry by 80% in 30 days." For Grable: "MAS 9 migration in 6 months — on AWS GovCloud, FedRAMP-aligned, SDVOSB set-aside eligible."
2. **Persona-based navigation paths** — separate "For Federal Buyers" and "For Commercial Buyers" entry points, with shared global nav but distinct conversion funnels.
3. **Multi-CTA layering** — top-of-page demo, mid-page free-assessment, sticky-footer "Get capability statement," bottom-page newsletter. Average B2B converts 2–4%; top-decile uses 4+ distinct CTAs matched to funnel stage.
4. **Trust stack** — security badges (SOC 2, ISO 27001, FedRAMP-aligned), client logos, certifications, real customer testimonials with name + title + photo. Stock photos are now a trust-killer (multiple 2026 sources flag this explicitly).
5. **Real product visuals over illustrations** — animated dashboards, product screenshots, build logs, deployment animations.

### Hero pattern trends 2026

- **Light backgrounds are the 2026 default** (white or gray-50), with dark heroes reserved for premium positioning.
- **Animated gradients, WebGL, shader-based heroes** — Stripe and Vercel set the gold standard. Stripe runs a skewed WebGL mesh gradient in purple/pink/cyan/orange; Vercel uses subtle animated gradients with whitespace.
- **Product-in-the-hero** — Vercel shows an animated build log; Linear's hero has a Codex agent actually picking up a real issue. The product *works* in the hero.
- **Editorial typography heroes** — asymmetric, type-first, big-statement copy with negative space doing the work.
- **Split-screen hero** — title/CTA on one side, promotional video or product demo on the other (Kearney centennial, S Kaba Consulting).
- **AI demo in hero** — for consulting firms, an interactive "ask our AI" widget that demonstrates expertise rather than describes it.

### Page length and scroll patterns

- **Homepages: 4-6 viewport scrolls.** Hero → outcome-proof (logos/stats) → service taxonomy → industry/use-case grid → testimonials → CTAs/footer.
- **Service pages: 1,800–3,500 words** with H2-driven topical depth, FAQ schema, and at least one comparison table.
- **Dual-audience pattern:** Global nav uses **task language** ("Migrate," "Host," "Modernize"), then **audience-specific signposting** on landing pages. The hybrid (task-nav + audience-signpost) outperforms pure "For X / For Y" splits in usability data.

### Conversion architecture — top-firm CTA layering

The top-decile pattern: a **conversion ladder** that lets a buyer self-select their commitment level on the same page:

1. **Low-friction:** Newsletter / "Get capability statement" (federal) / Resource download
2. **Medium-friction:** "Free Maximo migration assessment" / "Calculator" / "Compare hosting options"
3. **High-friction:** "Schedule a consultation" / "Talk to our SDVOSB lead"
4. **Dynamic CTAs** based on visitor behavior, industry, CRM stage (Mutiny, HubSpot, Drift personalization).

### Exemplary modern sites (2026 references)

- **Stripe, Vercel, Linear** — the SaaS canon. WebGL heroes, product-in-the-hero animation, ruthless above-the-fold positioning.
- **Slalom** — consulting site leading with an AI Research Report hero, split-diagonal design with professional portraiture.
- **Kearney** — centennial dramatic purple hero blending executive portraiture with broadcast-style imagery.
- **1898 & Co. / Burns & McDonnell** — explicitly cited in Maximo-adjacent EAM consulting; sharp dual-audience navigation.

### Failure modes that make a site "feel 2018"

- Rotating hero carousels (autoplay creates friction, kills accessibility).
- Generic stock photography of suit-people-shaking-hands.
- Vague value propositions ("We deliver enterprise solutions").
- Center-aligned identical-cards service grids with no hierarchy.
- "Solutions" mega-menus without persona signposting.
- No FAQ schema, no comparison tables, no calculators.
- Single sitewide CTA ("Contact Us") with no funnel-stage layering.
- No real customer voice — testimonials are quoted but anonymized or generic.
- Trust signals buried in the footer instead of in the hero.

## Thread 4 — Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) for Maximo Queries

### Who AI engines cite today for Maximo queries

When ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews answer "best IBM Maximo consultant," "Maximo as a Service provider," "MAS 9 migration partner," or "SDVOSB Maximo," they pull from a consistent source pool:

- **IBM.com / IBM PartnerPlus directory** — the primary authoritative source (Wikipedia of the Maximo world, in AI terms).
- **Naviam.io / projetech.com** — cited for "world's largest" claims and EAM-as-a-Service definitions.
- **Interlocsolutions.com** — cited for managed services and consulting service definitions.
- **Camcode "25 Best Maximo Software Consultants"** — heavily cited because listicle format is AI-quotable.
- **Starboard-consulting.com** — cited for Gold Partner credentialing.
- **MaxIron, MaxStack, Pragma Edge** — cited for migration tactical content.
- **AWS, IBM blogs** — cited for "Maximo on AWS" / FedRAMP / hosting architecture.
- **PRNewswire / IBM Newsroom** — cited for the April 2026 FedRAMP announcement (the canonical source for that story).

### Why these sites get cited

AI engines select via four signals (Discovered Labs, Yext, Frase, Whitehat SEO triangulation):

1. **Authority** — domain authority 80-100 accounts for 31.5% of all AI citations.
2. **Relevance** — semantic match to the query.
3. **Recency** — Perplexity favors content updated within 30 days (3.2× more citations) and within 12 months (community-validated).
4. **Structural clarity** — FAQ schema, comparison tables, definitions, quotable single-sentence statistics.

Platform-specific quirks:

- **ChatGPT** — Wikipedia accounts for ~47.9% of its top-10 cited sources. Encyclopedic, well-structured content wins.
- **Perplexity** — Reddit accounts for ~46.7% of top citations. Community validation and recency matter more.
- **Claude** — most cautious citation behavior; relies on trained knowledge, less on real-time browsing.
- **Google AI Overviews** — heavy E-E-A-T weight, author expertise, structured data.
- **Cross-platform overlap is small:** Only 11% of sites cited by ChatGPT are also cited by Perplexity. **Multi-platform citation is a long game.**

### llms.txt and structured data tactics

- **llms.txt** — emerging root-level file (yoursite.com/llms.txt) that declares site structure for AI crawlers. Adoption is early but accelerating; publishing one is a low-cost, high-signal move.
- **schema.org markup** — FAQPage, Organization, Service, Product, BreadcrumbList. **FAQPage schema correlates with the highest AI citation density** of any single structural choice.
- **Entity definitions** — explicit "What is Maximo as a Service?" definition blocks with 40-60 word answer capsules are quotable by every major LLM.
- **Comparison tables** — top-quartile pages with comparison tables get cited 8.4× more than the bottom half (segmentseo, averi.ai).
- **Brand mention signals** — getting mentioned (not necessarily linked) on authoritative sites (IBM blogs, industry analyst reports, Camcode-style listicles, federal contracting news) is a primary AI authority signal.

### Content formats AI engines prefer

1. **FAQ schema with one H3 per question and answer-in-first-sentence.** Highest citation density.
2. **Comparison tables** — vs Naviam, vs Interloc, vs hosting model, vs MAS license tier. Make the table machine-readable (proper `<table>` markup, no images).
3. **Definitive lists** — "Top 10," "5 things," "The 7 stages of." Listicles are AI-quotable.
4. **Expert quotes with attribution** — name, title, organization, date. AI engines preferentially cite content with named human authority.
5. **Statistical statements as single quotable sentences** — "Grable Services has migrated 800 million square feet of Maximo facility data across 47 enterprise clients."

### How a small SDVOSB can earn AI citations against Naviam and Cohesive

Five tactics, all triple-verified across the GEO research:

1. **Win the FedRAMP / SDVOSB / IL2-IL6 search slice first.** Naviam doesn't own this — they own commercial. Grable can dominate "FedRAMP Maximo," "SDVOSB Maximo Gold Partner," "MAS 9 on AWS GovCloud" through topical depth Naviam won't write.
2. **Publish the canonical comparison content.** "Maximo as a Service: Grable vs Naviam vs IBM MaaS — full comparison" with a real, fair, data-driven comparison table. AI engines cite the comparison page far more often than the marketing page.
3. **Publish llms.txt and rich schema across every page.** Most Maximo competitors have not yet adopted llms.txt — a one-week project that produces multi-quarter citation lift.
4. **Build the FAQ database.** 50-100 FAQ-schema-marked questions covering the full Maximo migration lifecycle. Each FAQ becomes a potential AI citation surface.
5. **Earn Reddit and community mentions.** Perplexity's 46.7% Reddit dependency means Grable should ensure honest, helpful presence in r/IBMMaximo, r/govcon, r/sysadmin Maximo threads. Citations from /r/IBMMaximo flow directly into Perplexity answers.

The investigative evidence converges on a single, narrow, defensible market position:

> **Grable Services is the SDVOSB IBM Maximo Gold Partner that runs MAS 9 on AWS GovCloud at FedRAMP-aligned compliance — for federal agencies and Fortune 500 enterprises who need migration certainty before the 2025 EOL is fully past and the 2026 FedRAMP era begins.**

Naviam owns "world's largest." IBM owns "FedRAMP Moderate baseline." Interloc owns "managed services depth." **No competitor owns the SDVOSB + Gold Partner + FedRAMP-aligned + commercial-marquee (Caesars, Sony, Xerox, Genentech) intersection.** Grable can.

The website revamp should be built to win exactly that intersection — through dual-audience navigation, FedRAMP-front-and-center messaging, FAQ + comparison schema for AI citation, modern hero patterns (animated product/build log style), and a conversion ladder that lets federal CO buyers and commercial VPs both self-select their path.
