**Prepared for:** Grable Services website revamp

**Scope:** 8 IBM Maximo / EAM consulting and hosting competitors
**Goal:** Build a "what good looks like" reference and identify differentiation opportunities

## Executive Note on Data Quality

Two domains supplied in the brief turned out to be stale or wrong:

- **naviam.com** — NXDOMAIN. The actual site is **naviam.io**. Naviam was formed in **2024 as a merger of nine independent Maximo firms** (ActiveG, BPD Zenith, EAM Swiss, InterPro Solutions, Lexco, Peacock Engineering, **Projetech**, Sharptree, ZNAPZ). That means *Projetech is now a sub-brand of Naviam* — the two URLs in the brief are effectively the same company at two stages of its rebrand. Projetech.com is still live and runs the old positioning while naviam.io carries the new umbrella brand.
- **interloc.com** — Parked on a GoDaddy "lander." The live site is **interlocsolutions.com**.
- **themaximoguys.com** — Parked on Hostinger DNS. The live site is **themaximoguys.ai**.

These three findings alone are a useful design signal for Grable: getting the `.com`/right-domain story right matters, and rebrands leave dead trails that look unprofessional in Google.

# Synthesis

## Top 5 IA / Navigation Patterns That Recur

1. **Services dropdown is the heaviest single menu.** Everyone hangs 5–9 service items off one parent. Cohesive uses verbs ("Upgrade / Optimize / Implement / Host"), the rest use nouns. Verbs win.
2. **Industries as a top-level nav item.** 7 of 8 promote Industries to the top nav — this is table stakes. Naviam (9), Cohesive (11), TheMaximoGuys (12) lead on industry depth.
3. **Resources / Learn as a parent category for blog + case studies + ebooks.** Universal pattern. No one calls it just "Blog." Always umbrella-named (Resources, Learn, Information).
4. **Products treated as a separate top-level when proprietary IP exists.** Naviam, Interloc, iMaxeam, and TheMaximoGuys all split Products from Services. Cohesive, A3J, Banetti, Projetech bury products inside Services. Splitting them out is a credibility signal — it says "we build, we don't just bill hours."
5. **About Us dropdown carries Careers + Press + Leadership + sometimes a learning academy.** Almost universal. Cohesive has a "Cohesive Academy" — turning About into a recruiting + thought-leadership funnel.

## Top 5 Design Patterns That Recur

1. **Hero with one short promise + one button + one secondary link.** Cohesive ("most value from IBM MAS"), Interloc ("Zero Downtime Upgrade"), Naviam ("Streamlined"), TheMaximoGuys ("Revolutionize"). Short, declarative.
2. **Three-pillar service section directly below hero.** Universal. Each pillar has an icon, a 3–8 word title, a sentence, and a "Learn more / Get started" link. Interloc and Naviam do this best.
3. **Client logo strip as social proof.** 6 of 8 use one. Cohesive (DPWorld, Con Edison), Naviam (TAQA, DHL), Projetech (Del-Co Water, Memorial Hospital) all do it well. Banetti and iMaxeam don't and look weaker for it.
4. **Blue is the default color.** 6 of 8 use blue as primary. **Naviam (green)** and **TheMaximoGuys (dark mode with navy)** stand out specifically because they don't conform.
5. **Real industrial photography, not stock illustration.** Naviam, Interloc, Cohesive all use workers-in-PPE, plant-floor, and control-room photos. Banetti uses metaphor imagery (pit crews, golf) and looks dated for it. The pattern is clear: real industrial people > clip art.

## Top 3 Conversion / CTA Strategies That Recur

1. **"Get Started" + "Talk to an expert" duo.** Almost universal: one low-friction CTA (Get Started → form), one high-intent CTA (Talk to an expert → calendar / phone). Naviam, Cohesive, Interloc, Projetech, A3J, TheMaximoGuys all use this pattern.
2. **Repeating the same CTA next to every service pillar.** Interloc does this most clearly — "Get Started" appears next to Consulting, next to Mobility, next to Hosting. Three different intents, same button text, scoped to context.
3. **Free trial / sandbox offer as the high-converting move.** Only Projetech ("Try MAS") and TheMaximoGuys ("Try Our AI Assist") have a true low-friction trial. This is the single biggest gap in the category — and the biggest opportunity for Grable.

## Three Things NONE of Them Do Well That Grable Could Differentiate On

1. **Federal / SDVOSB credentials displayed prominently.** Interloc and TheMaximoGuys *sell* to federal but don't display UEI, CAGE, SDVOSB, NAICS, or GSA Schedule on their public sites. Projetech leads with FedRAMP but stops short of the full federal-vendor disclosure. **If Grable Services is SDVOSB-certified and federally registered, the website should put UEI, CAGE, SDVOSB badge, NAICS codes, and GSA Schedule (if any) in the footer of every page AND on a dedicated "Federal" capability page.** None of the competitors do this. It is the cleanest differentiator in the field for any federal-eligible buyer who needs to filter vendors.
2. **Transparent pricing.** Only Projetech hints at pricing (a "Pricing" link buried in a dropdown). No competitor publishes a tier matrix, an hourly range, or a "starting at" anchor for MaaS or consulting. A published pricing page — even directional ("MaaS starts at $X/month for up to N users; Consulting starts at $Y/day") — is a buyer-respect move that almost no one is making. It also screens out unqualified leads and saves sales time.
3. **An honest assessment / self-serve diagnostic tool.** A3J has the "MAS Readiness Hub" idea but it's a content hub, not a tool. Nobody offers a real interactive Maximo health check, license-optimization estimator, MAS-9 upgrade-cost estimator, or hosting-vs-on-prem TCO calculator. A 5-minute interactive tool that produces a personalized PDF would be the single highest-converting asset in the category.

**Honorable mention** — none of them have a podcast worth subscribing to (Cohesive has one but it's not promoted hard). The audio-content lane is wide open for a small firm with a strong personality.

## The Single Best Site Overall

**Naviam (naviam.io).** Reasons:

- Cleanest IA: 7 top-level items, each with a clear job-to-be-done. Asset Management as the lead nav item (not Services or Products) signals customer-first thinking.
- Best visual differentiation in the category — the green accent against an all-blue field is a small choice that carries a lot of brand weight.
- Strongest trust signal stack: Platinum + ISO 27001/27017 + SOC 2 + Cyber Essentials Plus + 400+ professionals + 9-firm merger story + 1,000 managed environments. Nothing else comes close.
- Real industrial photography that's clearly licensed/original, not stock.
- They sell their proprietary IP (DataStudio, Opqo, EZMaxMobile) as products with their own pages — services and software are split.
- Their merger story is told as a strength, not a hand-wave. That's hard to do well.

**Honorable mention:** TheMaximoGuys (themaximoguys.ai) has the sharpest *positioning* in the field — "Agentic AI for Maximo" is a category-defining bet — but Naviam wins on overall execution, scale signals, and IA discipline.

**The site to avoid emulating:** Banetti. Dated visuals, racing metaphors that don't land, thin content, no client logos, no certifications display, and the industries claimed in the brief (healthcare, government) aren't actually surfaced on the public site.

## Direct Implications for Grable Services

1. **Pick a verb-led services nav** (Upgrade · Optimize · Implement · Host · Support) like Cohesive. Avoid noun-only menus.
2. **Lead the hero with one promise**, not four value props. Examples that work: "Zero Downtime Upgrade." "Maximo as a Service. Done Right." "Federal Maximo, FedRAMP Ready."
3. **Display federal credentials in the footer of every page** and on a dedicated Federal page (UEI, CAGE, SDVOSB badge, NAICS list, GSA Schedule if any, FedRAMP status). This is the single biggest gap in the category.
4. **Build one interactive tool** — MAS 9 readiness check OR hosting TCO calculator OR license-optimization estimator. Promote it from the homepage hero.
5. **Publish at least directional pricing.** "MaaS starts at $X/user/month" is enough.
6. **Productize what's productizable.** Even if Grable is mostly services today, if there's a deployment template, a migration tool, or an automation script set — make it a named asset with its own page.
7. **Choose a color that isn't generic IBM-blue.** Naviam went green and it pays off. A muted but distinctive palette (green, teal, deep purple, or a strong neutral) sets the brand apart immediately.
8. **Real industrial photography only.** Workers in PPE, control rooms, plant floors. No racing metaphors, no clip-art handshakes.
9. **One sub-brand for federal** (e.g., "Grable Federal" or "Grable Gov") with its own landing page even if it's only a vertical of the main offering. Federal buyers want to feel like they're buying from a federal-focused vendor.
10. **Own the right domain story.** Grable should own .com, .io, and .ai variants and redirect them all to one canonical site — none of the parked-domain mess that themaximoguys.com and naviam.com show.
